Introduction

Our data was originally collected as part of a group assignment in the Data Analysis 1 course and includes 26-26 observations of the price in HUF of two products: regular Coca Cola 0.5l plastic bottle and Nutella 400g. In case of Coke, the range of prices was between 219 and 390 HUF with an average of 279.33 HUF, while Nutella prices fell into the range between 1099 and 1419 HUF with an average of 1272.05 HUF.

Data are available from: https://github.com/kanyipi/DA1/blob/main/bogota_data.csv

Data

The collection was carried out by anonymous visit to randomly selected stores in districts 9 and 16. (Three stores are on the edge of districts 8 and 9. But we decided to keep the data in the table as they are still valuable for our current analysis purpose.) Apart from that, we measured the busyness of the stores using number of people waiting in queue divided by the number of cashiers. In addition, the data table also includes information on the position of both products on the shelf and if they had a discount at the time of the visit, for these we used the observation method. As a complement we include qualitative information about the store (name, type, address, district and nationality).

Conclusion

  • The distribution of Nutella prices is way more spread out than Coke prices with over twice the standard deviation. With less missing values in Coke prices, we can tell stores are more likely to sell Coke than Nutella.
  • The conditional mean of busyness is a bit lower in inner city than outer city. However, the median are the same. And the distribution of busyness in outer city is more spread out with double the IQR as shown in the boxplot, whereas it is more centralized in inner city.
